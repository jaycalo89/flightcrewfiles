Pilot Report
2004USS Nimitz Carrier GroupPacific Ocean

The "Tic Tac" That Outran an F/A-18

— November 2004

Navy fighter pilots training off the California coast were vectored toward a radar contact descending from 80,000 feet to sea level in seconds. Pilots described a smooth, white, tic-tac-shaped object with no visible wings, exhaust or control surfaces that mirrored their aircraft's movements before accelerating away faster than their jet could track. The encounter was later confirmed by the Department of Defense as authentic radar and cockpit footage.

1986Commercial Flight CrewAlaska

The Object That Shadowed a Cargo Jet For 400 Miles

— November 1986

Japan Air Lines Flight 1628's captain, Kenji Terauchi, and his crew reported large objects pacing their 747 cargo jet over Alaska, visible on both onboard radar and ground radar, for roughly 400 miles before they broke off. The FAA confirmed the radar returns and found the crew credible; the case remains officially unresolved.

The Disc Over Gate C17

— November 2006

United Airlines employees and pilots reported a dark, saucer-shaped object hovering silently over a gate before ascending straight up through solid cloud cover, punching a visible hole in the overcast behind it. The airline directed staff not to discuss it; the FAA said no radar data was reviewed because no incident was formally reported at the time.

2014Navy Helicopter CrewOff the Chilean Coast

The FLIR Footage Chile's Government Couldn't Explain

— November 2014

A Chilean Navy helicopter crew recorded infrared footage of an object flying alongside their aircraft, emitting no heat signature consistent with any known aircraft. Chile's official committee for the study of anomalous aerial phenomena examined the footage for two years and classified the object as genuinely unidentified.

1976Air Force InterceptorsTehran

The Object That Disabled Fighter Jet Instruments

— September 1976

Iranian Air Force F-4 Phantoms scrambled to intercept a bright object over Tehran reported losing weapons and communications systems each time they approached it, with systems returning to normal only once they backed away — including Major Parviz Jafari's missile system going dead as he tried to fire. The incident is documented in a declassified Defense Intelligence Agency report.

1952Air Traffic Control & InterceptorsWashington, D.C.

The Weekend Unidentified Objects Flew Over the Capital

— July 1952

Over two consecutive weekends, radar operators at National Airport and nearby military facilities tracked multiple fast-moving unidentified objects over restricted airspace. Fighter jets were scrambled twice; pilots described lights that outran their aircraft with no accompanying radar-correlated aircraft ever identified.

Cover Story Confirmed
Jul 1947U.S. Army Air FieldRoswell, New Mexico

The Crash That Started It All

— July 1947

After ranch foreman Mac Brazel found strange metallic debris scattered across a New Mexico ranch, the local Army Air Field's press office announced it had recovered a "flying disc" — a release retracted within 24 hours in favor of a weather balloon explanation. The U.S. Air Force's own 1994–1997 investigations later concluded the wreckage actually came from a classified program called Project Mogul, and that the weather-balloon story was itself a cover story to protect that secret. Roswell remains the most famous UFO case in the world, and the source of enduring claims — never substantiated in any official record — that bodies were recovered and witnesses were pressured into silence.

Military Documented
Dec 1980U.S. Air Force PersonnelRendlesham Forest, England

The Craft In The Forest Beside The NATO Base

— December 1980

Over several nights in late December 1980, U.S. Air Force personnel stationed at RAF Woodbridge — adjacent to a NATO airbase — reported strange lights and a craft on the ground in the surrounding forest. Deputy base commander Lt. Col. Charles Halt led a follow-up patrol, narrated the events in real time on a micro-cassette recorder, and later documented radiation readings at the site notably above background level in a memo sent to the UK Ministry of Defence. It remains the most significant UAP case ever documented by U.S. military personnel on British soil.

Mar 1997Thousands Of WitnessesPhoenix, Arizona

The Night A Governor Watched, Then Denied It

— March 1997

On the evening of March 13, 1997, thousands of people across Arizona reported a mile-wide, V-shaped formation of lights moving silently and slowly over Phoenix for roughly two hours. Witnesses ranged from ordinary residents to police officers and pilots, and then-Governor Fife Symington publicly mocked the sighting at a press conference — only admitting a decade later that he, too, had personally witnessed the object and regretted the joke. It remains one of the largest mass UFO sightings in American history.

1989–1990Belgian Air ForceBelgium

The Wave Of Triangles Over Belgium

— 1989–1990

Beginning in late 1989, thousands of witnesses across Belgium reported a wave of large, silent, triangular craft with bright lights, culminating on the night of March 30, 1990, when Belgian Air Force F-16s were scrambled after a target was picked up on multiple radar systems. The jets recorded brief radar locks during nine attempted intercepts, though Belgium's military later attributed most of those contacts to weather anomalies and the aircraft locking onto each other. It remains one of the most extensively radar- and visually-documented UFO waves ever investigated by a national government.

Official Government Filing
Oct 1967Royal Canadian NavyShag Harbour, Nova Scotia

The Only UFO Crash A Government Ever Filed As One

— October 1967

On the night of October 4, 1967, at least eleven witnesses in Shag Harbour, Nova Scotia watched a large lit object descend into the Atlantic and disappear beneath the surface, leaving floating debris and a foam slick behind. The Royal Canadian Navy dispatched a dedicated Fleet Diving Unit to search the seafloor over several days, and found no trace of wreckage. It remains one of the only incidents in the world formally filed by a national government as an unidentified flying object rather than resolved as anything conventional.

FAA Radar Confirmed
Jan 2008Hundreds Of WitnessesStephenville, Texas

The Object The Air Force Changed Its Story About

— January 2008

On the evening of January 8, 2008, hundreds of residents of Stephenville, Texas reported a massive, fast-moving, silent object crossing the sky, followed by what witnesses described as military jets in pursuit. The Air Force initially denied having any aircraft in the area that night before later confirming nearly a dozen F-16s were on a training mission, and a subsequent analysis of FAA radar data obtained via a Freedom of Information Act request showed an unidentified target moving at speeds and on a flight path inconsistent with the confirmed military traffic. The case remains one of the few UAP sightings backed by released government radar data.
